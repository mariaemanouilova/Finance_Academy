Investment Basics

Investing is putting your money into financial assets with the goal of growing your wealth over time. The key is understanding different investment vehicles and how to match them with your goals.

Types of Investments

  • Stocks: Ownership shares in companies with growth potential
  • Bonds: Loans to companies or governments that pay fixed interest
  • Exchange-Traded Funds (ETFs): Diversified portfolios that trade like stocks
  • Mutual Funds: Professionally managed portfolios of securities
  • Real Estate: Property investment with rental income potential

Diversification & Risk Management

Don't put all your eggs in one basket. Diversify across asset classes, sectors, and geographies to reduce risk. Consider your risk tolerance and time horizon when building your investment portfolio.

Long-Term Strategy

The power of compound growth means starting early and staying invested matters. Develop a strategy aligned with your goals and review it regularly, but avoid emotional decisions based on short-term market fluctuations.
