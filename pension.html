Pension

Plan for retirement: pension types, contribution strategies, and tax considerations.

Retirement Planning Overview

Retirement planning is one of the most important financial goals. The earlier you start, the more time your investments have to grow through compound returns.

Types of Pensions & Retirement Accounts

  • 401(k) Plans: Employer-sponsored plans with employer matching contributions. Contributions are tax-deferred.
  • IRA (Individual Retirement Account): Personal retirement accounts available in Traditional (tax-deferred) and Roth (tax-free) versions.
  • Pension Plans: Defined benefit plans that guarantee a monthly income in retirement.
  • SIMPLE IRA: Designed for small business owners and self-employed individuals.
  • SEP IRA: Allows employers to contribute to employee retirement accounts with higher contribution limits.

Contribution Strategy

Maximize employer matching in 401(k) plans first. Then contribute to an IRA. Always take advantage of tax-deferred growth opportunities. Increase contributions annually as your income grows.

Tax Considerations

Understand the tax implications of different accounts. Traditional plans offer immediate tax deductions, while Roth accounts provide tax-free withdrawals in retirement. Consider your current and expected retirement tax brackets.
